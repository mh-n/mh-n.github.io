Research Projects

Human-Centered AI in Healthcare

Designing adaptive, human-centered AI systems to better mitigate potential harm and maximize benefit for all users. We must adhere to validated clinical standards while also effectively serving patients and clinicians whose preferences vary widely. My long-term goal is to study how we may create usable, practical AI solutions for patients and providers, informed by pluralistic alignment methods.

EHR Data & Clinical Genetics

Characterizing and augmenting clinical genetics care through structured and unstructured electronic health record (EHR) data. I have designed and developed machine learning and clinical natural language processing algorithms to maximize usable information from the EHR. Projects include:

  • Detection of clinically useful phenotypic features from clinical notes of patients suspected of rare Mendelian disorders.
  • Automated extraction of efficiency measures from genetic counseling notes, reducing annotation time.
  • Knowledge-graph augmented extraction of family health history from diverse clinical and consumer data sources.

Consumer-Facing Digital Health Chatbots

Developing consumer-facing digital health chatbots to empower consumers to understand and act on their health. Projects include:

  • KIT: A novel consumer-facing flow-based chatbot for family history (FHx) data collection, assessing usability, engagement, and report usefulness.
  • Strolr: A retrieval-augmented-generation chatbot to answer consumer health pregnancy queries, with design informed by semi-structured interviews of key pregnancy and clinical stakeholders.
